# 5 Ways Pharmacy Software Can Help Reduce Phone Calls

Phone calls are a necessary part of running an independent pharmacy, but they can also take up a significant amount of staff time. Patients call to ask if prescriptions are ready, request refills, check prices, confirm hours, ask about delivery, or follow up on medication availability.

While some calls will always require a personal conversation, many routine calls can be reduced with the right pharmacy software. Modern pharmacy management tools help automate communication, improve visibility, and give patients more convenient ways to get the information they need.

Here are five ways pharmacy software can help independent pharmacies reduce phone calls and keep staff focused on higher-value tasks.

## 1. Send Automated Prescription Notifications

One of the most common reasons patients call the pharmacy is to ask, “Is my prescription ready?” When staff have to answer this question over and over throughout the day, it can interrupt workflow and slow down prescription processing.

Automated prescription notifications help solve this problem by keeping patients informed without requiring a manual phone call. Pharmacy software can send text or email alerts when prescriptions are ready for pickup, when a refill is due, or when additional action is needed.

BestRx includes patient messaging tools that allow pharmacies to send customizable text and email alerts manually or automatically. By proactively updating patients, pharmacies can reduce routine status-check calls while improving the patient experience.

## 2. Make Refill Requests Easier for Patients

Refill requests can create a steady stream of phone calls, especially for patients who take maintenance medications. When patients have to call the pharmacy every time they need a refill, staff must stop what they are doing to look up the patient, review the prescription, and process the request.

Pharmacy software can help simplify refill management by giving patients more convenient ways to request refills and helping staff manage those requests more efficiently. Automated refill tools can also help pharmacies stay ahead of routine medication needs.

BestRx supports automated refill workflows that help independent pharmacies reduce repetitive manual tasks. With better refill management, pharmacies can spend less time handling refill calls and more time serving patients in person.

## 3. Use Two-Way Text Messaging for Quick Questions

Not every patient question requires a phone conversation. Sometimes patients simply need to confirm a pickup time, respond to a reminder, ask about a refill, or provide a quick update.

Two-way text messaging gives pharmacies a faster, more convenient way to communicate with patients. Instead of relying only on phone calls and voicemail, pharmacy teams can answer simple questions through text and keep communication moving more efficiently.

BestRx offers two-way patient messaging, allowing patients to respond to text notifications. This gives pharmacies a practical way to handle quick communication without tying up phone lines or pulling staff away from other work.

## 4. Improve Prescription Workflow Visibility

Calls often happen when patients are unsure what is going on with their prescriptions. They may call to ask whether a prescription was received, whether it is being filled, whether there is an issue, or whether it is ready.

Pharmacy software can help reduce confusion by improving internal workflow visibility. When staff can quickly see prescription status, refill activity, patient details, and next steps, they can work more efficiently and provide clearer communication when patients do reach out.

BestRx is designed with pharmacist-friendly workflows, including single-screen viewing, patient profile management, prescription verification tools, and responsive search capabilities. These tools help staff find information quickly, reduce internal delays, and keep prescriptions moving through the pharmacy.

## 5. Give Staff Better Reporting and Follow-Up Tools

Some phone calls happen because important follow-up tasks are being tracked manually or inconsistently. Staff may need to call patients about unclaimed prescriptions, refill opportunities, adherence gaps, or other routine follow-ups.

Reporting and workflow tools can help pharmacies better organize these tasks. Instead of relying on memory, sticky notes, or manual lists, pharmacy teams can use reporting tools to identify what needs attention and prioritize outreach more effectively.

BestRx includes reporting and analytics tools that help pharmacies access important operational data. With better insight into pharmacy activity, staff can take a more organized approach to patient follow-up, reduce unnecessary back-and-forth, and spend less time reacting to preventable phone calls.

## The Bottom Line

Phone calls will always be part of pharmacy care, but they should not overwhelm the workday. With the right pharmacy software, independent pharmacies can reduce routine calls, automate patient communication, streamline refill requests, and improve prescription workflow visibility.
